Demonstratives

Choice depends on two things: is it singular or plural, and is it near or far from you?

Distance Singular Plural
Near This These
Far That Those
This pen (in my hand) is better than that pen (over there). These keys (here) open those doors (across the room).

Imperatives

Used for commands, instructions, and requests. No subject is needed — start with the verb.

Affirmative — base verb

Close the door.

Sit down, please.

Negative — Don't + base verb

Don't close the door.

Don't be late.

Common mistake: "Don't running" — the verb after Don't must stay in its base form: "Don't run."

Module 2 · Unit 3

Present Continuous & Present Simple

What's happening right now vs. what happens generally — and the verbs that break the rule.

Present Continuous: Subject + be + V-ing

I am (I'm) He/She/It is ('s) We/You/They are ('re)

V-ing spelling rules:

RuleExample
Drop silent -emake → making
1 syllable, CVC → double consonantstop → stopping
-ie → ylie → lying
otherwise: just add -ingread → reading

Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Present Simple

Habits, routines, facts.

Time words: always, usually, every day, on Mondays

She walks to school every day.

Present Continuous

Actions happening right now.

Time words: now, right now, at the moment, look!

She is walking to school right now.

Stative Verbs — No -ing Allowed

Verbs describing states, feelings, or thoughts (like, want, know, need, love, believe, understand, own) are almost never used in the continuous form.

Incorrect

I am wanting some coffee.

She is knowing the answer.

Correct

I want some coffee.

She knows the answer.

Module 3 · Unit 4

Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

Comparing two things vs. picking the extreme of a whole group.

Two things

Comparative

adjective + -er  or  more + adjective  +  than

A train is faster than a bike.

A whole group

Superlative

the + adjective + -est  or  the most + adjective

The bullet train is the fastest.

Spelling Rules

Adjective type Comparative Superlative
1 syllable — tall taller the tallest
1 syllable, CVC — big bigger the biggest
2 syllables, ending -y — happy happier the happiest
2+ syllables — interesting more interesting the most interesting
